How I'd turn a good product into a store that actually sells.

Most DTC stores don't have a traffic problem — they have a clarity, trust and friction problem. Here's a full landing page and product page rebuilt around buyer psychology, with every decision explained. Flip on CRO Insights to see the thinking behind each move.

The brief

A real product, a leaking funnel

VERDANT is a daily greens & adaptogen blend — good product, real demand, active ads. But the store talks like a label, not like a person. Here's the difference between the version that loses visitors and the version designed to convert them.

Before — what most stores do

The funnel leaks at every step

  • Hero just states the brand & product name — no outcome, no reason to care
  • No trust signals above the fold — a skeptical buyer has no anchor
  • Generic "Shop Now" button, low contrast, easy to ignore
  • Price shown raw ($59) with no context — feels expensive
  • Reviews buried at the bottom, far from the buying decision
  • Wall-of-text description; ingredients dumped in a paragraph
  • No money-back guarantee near the CTA — the last doubt wins
  • Not designed for the thumb, where 60–70% of traffic lives
After — designed to convert

Every doubt answered before it's asked

  • Outcome-first headline a buyer self-identifies with in one read
  • Trust bar above the fold — credibility in the first 3 seconds
  • High-contrast, outcome-based CTA that's impossible to miss
  • Price reframed as cost-per-day; subscription as the smart default
  • Star rating and reviews placed at the decision point
  • Scannable benefit bullets with felt outcomes and timeframes
  • Risk reversal next to the button — the last objection removed
  • Mobile-first layout with a sticky add-to-cart
The framework

A first-time visitor silently asks the same five questions. If the page doesn't answer one of them, that's where they leave. The whole rebuild is organized around closing all five — fast.
